Technology in Education — Vision

📖 Est. reading time: 4 min

Technology will be used at all levels in education — from improving classroom processes, to supporting teacher professional development, to enhancing educational planning and management. A National Educational Technology Forum (NETF) will be created to provide a platform for the free exchange of ideas on the use of technology to enhance learning, assessment, planning, administration, and so on, both for school and higher education.

"The focus of technology-based interventions will always be on improving learning and shall augment the role of the teacher rather than replace it."

— NEP 2020, Para 23.1

23.1 Vision for Technology Integration

Technology, as it advances, offers enormous opportunities for improving the quality and access of education in India. At the same time, rapid advances in technology also require citizens to have new 'digital literacy' and preparedness in order to take full advantage of and participate meaningfully in the technological revolution that is under way. Continuous review and deployment of the latest technologies to improve all aspects of education will be taken up appropriately, in a manner that helps achieve the national educational goals and targets including those of universal foundational literacy and numeracy, and universal access to quality education.

Key Technology Objectives

  • Improve classroom processes and engagement through technology
  • Support teacher professional development through digital means
  • Enhance educational planning and management through data systems
  • Provide high-quality e-content in regional languages
  • Develop new assessments through technology-based tools
  • Increase research using technology
  • Support students with disabilities through appropriate technology

Digital Literacy as a Core Competency

With the increasing prevalence of digital systems in everyday life, digital literacy — i.e., the ability to effectively and critically navigate, evaluate, and create information using a range of digital technologies — becomes a critical life skill for all students. Computational thinking and coding will be taught from an early stage, and issues around cybersafety and ethics will be appropriately incorporated into curricula and teaching practices.

Digital Education Vision Architecture

Digital Infrastructure

📖 Est. reading time: 3 min

An excellent digital infrastructure for education will be created by the Government. This will include reliable electricity, and internet connectivity at all schools across India. The goal will be to provide adequate connectivity to all schools by 2025.

23.2 Infrastructure Requirements

All schools shall aim to have reliable internet connectivity, with modern computer labs, digital devices (tablets, computers) available to students, and high-quality e-content available in regional and local languages as well as in English. The digital infrastructure will be built step by step, and the existing initiatives — such as the PM eVIDYA programme — will be used to accelerate the provision of digital resources to all learners.

  • Reliable electricity and internet connectivity at all schools
  • Modern computer laboratories with adequate hardware
  • Digital devices — tablets, computers — for all students
  • High-quality e-content in regional and local languages
  • Bandwidth provisioning aligned with student-teacher ratios
  • Smart classroom integration with interactive boards
  • School Management Systems (SMS) for administrative efficiency
Infrastructure provisioning shall be done in a phased manner, ensuring that schools in the most educationally backward blocks receive priority in the allocation of digital resources.

PM eVIDYA and Related Initiatives

PM eVIDYA — a multi-mode access programme to digital/online education — shall be leveraged extensively. It includes DIKSHA (one nation, one digital platform), TV channels (one class, one channel), Extensive use of Radio, Community Radio and CBSE Podcast — Shiksha Vani, and special e-content for differently-abled students.

Online & Blended Learning

📖 Est. reading time: 5 min

NEP 2020 envisions a rich ecosystem of online and blended learning, recognising that these approaches can substantially expand access to high quality education. Online and digital education will not be used to replace teachers or face-to-face interaction; instead, they will be used to augment and enrich the educational experience.

Blended Learning Framework

The blended model integrates online digital media with traditional classroom methods. Students and teachers interact both online and face-to-face, in a structured manner. NEP 2020 envisions that:

  • At least 25% of learning can be delivered online in secondary school
  • Curriculum planning will include structured online components
  • Assessment rubrics for online learning will be developed
  • Teacher training will include facilitating online learning
  • Regional language content will be prioritised
Blended Learning Model

23.5 Pilot Studies and Research

Pilot studies for online and blended modes of education will be conducted at all levels of education. These pilots will rigorously track learning outcomes, student engagement, equity of access, and other relevant metrics, in order to gather evidence on the efficacy of online and blended education. NETF will facilitate the review and dissemination of this research.

Virtual Labs

📖 Est. reading time: 3 min

Virtual labs will be set up to enable students in all parts of India — including the most remote regions — to have access to high-quality laboratory facilities and equipment. These virtual labs will be built to allow students to perform experiments, observe phenomena, and develop scientific temperament in an online environment.

Components of Virtual Labs

  • Simulation-based experiments for science subjects (Physics, Chemistry, Biology)
  • Interactive animations demonstrating complex concepts
  • Step-by-step guided experiment procedures
  • Data recording and analysis tools integrated within the platform
  • Assessment components to test understanding of lab procedures
  • Teacher dashboard for monitoring student lab progress
  • Multilingual interface to support regional language learners
Virtual labs shall be especially beneficial for schools in rural, hilly, and remote areas where physical laboratory infrastructure may be limited or absent, ensuring no student is deprived of quality practical education.

Integration with Physical Labs

Virtual labs shall not replace physical laboratories but shall complement them. The virtual lab experience shall help students prepare for actual laboratory sessions by familiarising them with procedures, safety protocols, and expected outcomes before performing physical experiments. This blended approach shall enhance both conceptual understanding and practical skills.

Section 5
📘 Source: NEP 2020

National Educational Technology Forum (NETF)

📖 Est. reading time: 4 min

A National Educational Technology Forum (NETF) will be created as an autonomous body to provide a platform for the free exchange of ideas on the use of technology to enhance learning, assessment, planning, and administration — both for school and higher education.

23.4 Functions of NETF

NETF will:

  • Advise education ministries at all levels — Union and State — regarding the deployment of technology to enhance education
  • Build and deploy a digital education infrastructure for the country, working with DIKSHA and PM eVIDYA
  • Identify, pilot, evaluate, and scale up use of technology in education
  • Fund relevant research on technology and education
  • Conduct pilots and longitudinal studies in technology-based learning
  • Evaluate technologies and publish guidelines and standards for adoption
  • Create a National Repository of Open Educational Resources (NROER)
  • Work with technology companies, educational institutions, and government bodies to accelerate EdTech ecosystem development
NETF shall be an autonomous body with adequate funding, infrastructure, and professional expertise to independently advise on technology decisions affecting the entire national educational ecosystem.

Governance Structure

NETF will be governed by a board comprising technology experts, educational practitioners, curriculum specialists, assessment experts, and representatives from the States. It will publish annual reports on the state of technology in Indian education and make specific actionable recommendations to improve technology deployment across the system.

Digital Content Creation

📖 Est. reading time: 4 min

High-quality e-content shall be created in all regional languages and made available freely on digital platforms such as DIKSHA. Content creation will involve the collaboration of teachers, subject experts, instructional designers, and educational technologists.

Standards for Digital Content

  • Content shall align strictly with the National Curriculum Framework (NCF)
  • Each unit shall include learning objectives, content body, activities, and assessment
  • Content shall be developed at multiple levels of complexity to accommodate diverse learners
  • Accessibility standards shall be followed — content shall be available for differently-abled learners
  • Metadata standards shall be applied for interoperability with national repositories
  • Copyright and licensing policies shall be clearly stated for all digital content

Content Governance

A robust content governance mechanism shall ensure quality, accuracy, and currency of all digital educational content. Peer review, expert validation, and teacher feedback mechanisms shall be institutionalised as part of the content lifecycle management process.

Teachers shall be actively involved in content creation and review. Teacher-created content uploaded on DIKSHA shall be reviewed and, where appropriate, endorsed by the platform — recognising teachers as knowledge producers, not merely consumers of digital content.

📘 Source: NEP 2020

Teacher Digital Training

📖 Est. reading time: 4 min

Teachers will be empowered with the digital skills and tools required to use technology effectively in their teaching. Teacher training programmes — pre-service and in-service — shall include substantial components on digital competency, EdTech tool usage, online pedagogy, and data-driven teaching.

Digital Competency Framework for Teachers

  • Level 1 — Digital Foundational: Basic computer operation, internet usage, email, digital safety
  • Level 2 — Digital Classroom: Use of DIKSHA, smart boards, educational apps, digital content access
  • Level 3 — Digital Pedagogy: Designing blended learning, creating e-content, online assessment
  • Level 4 — Digital Leadership: Mentoring peers, leading digital transformation, research on EdTech
Teacher Digital Training Cycle

DIKSHA as Teacher Training Platform

DIKSHA shall serve as the primary platform for in-service teacher professional development in digital skills. The platform shall host courses, assessment tools, and certification pathways for teachers at all levels. Completion of digital training modules shall be linked to teacher performance appraisal systems.

📘 Source: NEP 2020

Inclusive Digital Access

📖 Est. reading time: 3 min

Technology must serve the goal of equity. NEP 2020 recognises that digital divides along lines of geography, gender, and socioeconomic status can exacerbate existing educational inequalities if not proactively addressed.

Strategies for Inclusive Digital Access

  • Priority provisioning of internet and devices to disadvantaged districts and schools
  • Community ICT centres and digital libraries in rural areas
  • Gender-sensitive design of digital learning platforms
  • Assistive technology for learners with disabilities (screen readers, audio descriptions, sign language)
  • Content in Scheduled Languages and tribal languages
  • Low-bandwidth versions of digital content for areas with poor connectivity
  • Offline content delivery mechanisms — USB, SDcard, local servers
"Special attention shall be given to ensuring that digital education does not bypass disadvantaged groups. Digital equity shall be treated as a policy imperative, not an afterthought."

— NEP 2020, Digital Education Framework

Overview of DIKSHA

📖 Est. reading time: 4 min

DIKSHA — Digital Infrastructure for Knowledge Sharing — is India's national platform for school education. It is built on the Sunbird open-source platform and is maintained by the National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT) under the Ministry of Education, Government of India.

Core Purpose

DIKSHA serves as a "one nation, one digital platform" for school education. It provides:

  • A unified digital platform for teachers, students, and parents
  • Access to curriculum-aligned educational content
  • Teacher professional development and training resources
  • QR-code linked digital content for printed textbooks
  • Capacity building and professional learning communities
  • Data and analytics for monitoring learning outcomes
DIKSHA Digital Ecosystem Architecture

Scale and Reach

DIKSHA has been adopted by all States and Union Territories of India. It provides content in more than 30 languages, serves millions of teachers, and provides access to lakhs of digital resources aligned with State and NCERT curricula. It operates under a federated architecture allowing States to maintain their own content portals within the DIKSHA framework.

Section 10
🟡 Source: DIKSHA

Teacher Resource Platform

📖 Est. reading time: 4 min

DIKSHA serves as the primary teacher resource and professional development platform for school teachers across India. The teacher-facing features of DIKSHA are designed to support continuous professional learning, content access, and peer collaboration.

Teacher Features on DIKSHA

  • Access to curriculum-aligned teaching resources for all grades and subjects
  • Video lessons, lesson plans, and pedagogical guides
  • Online courses for professional development (in-service training)
  • Certificates and digital badges upon course completion
  • QR-code scanning to access linked digital content from textbooks
  • Discussion forums and professional learning communities
  • State-specific content portals within the DIKSHA framework
  • Assessment tools for classroom use
  • Progress tracking for student and course completion
DIKSHA treats teachers as the central node of the educational ecosystem. All platform features are designed to reduce teacher workload, enhance pedagogical capacity, and create meaningful digital professional learning experiences.

Teacher Registration and Profile

Teachers register on DIKSHA using their official credentials. The platform creates a personalised profile that tracks professional development activities, course completions, content consumed, and contributions made. This profile can be shared with educational administrators for appraisal and professional recognition purposes.

Section 11
🟡 Source: DIKSHA

QR Code Textbook Integration

📖 Est. reading time: 4 min

DIKSHA pioneered the Energised Textbook initiative — also known as the QR-coded textbook or ETBM (Energised TextBook Model). Under this initiative, printed textbooks are embedded with QR codes that link to digital content on DIKSHA when scanned using any QR code scanner or the DIKSHA app.

What QR Codes Link To

  • Explanatory videos and animations for concepts in the chapter
  • Interactive simulations and virtual lab activities
  • Practice questions and formative assessment items
  • Teacher resources and pedagogical notes
  • Additional reading materials and reference resources
  • Multilingual content alternatives
QR Code Textbook Integration Flow

ETBM Impact and Scale

The Energised Textbook Model has been implemented across multiple States and Union Territories. Crores of textbooks have been linked to DIKSHA content through QR codes. The model has demonstrated significant improvement in student engagement with digital supplementary content, particularly for Science and Mathematics subjects.

Capacity Building Modules

📖 Est. reading time: 3 min

DIKSHA hosts a comprehensive suite of capacity building modules for teachers and school leaders. These modules are designed by NCERT, State Councils (SCERT/SIE), and other recognised academic bodies, and are made available on the platform for self-paced learning.

Categories of Capacity Building Modules

  • Subject-specific pedagogical training — modules covering content and pedagogical knowledge for each subject and grade level
  • Inclusive education — training on teaching students with special needs
  • Early childhood education — ECCE pedagogy and activity-based learning approaches
  • Assessment for learning — formative assessment strategies and tools
  • Social-emotional learning — student wellbeing and mental health approaches
  • Digital pedagogy — using technology effectively in classroom teaching
  • Leadership and management — school leadership development programmes
All capacity building modules on DIKSHA are freely accessible, self-paced, and available in multiple regional languages. Completion of modules is tracked and certificates are issued upon successful assessment.

Learning Management Features

📖 Est. reading time: 3 min

DIKSHA provides a comprehensive Learning Management System (LMS) that enables structured course delivery, progress tracking, assessment, and certification.

Key LMS Features

  • Course creation and content upload tools for content creators
  • Enrolment management — teachers and students can enrol in relevant courses
  • Progress tracking — learner dashboards showing course completion status
  • Automated assessment and result generation
  • Certificate generation upon successful course completion
  • Batch management for cohort-based training programmes
  • Notification systems for course deadlines, new content, and announcements
  • Discussion boards for learner peer interaction
  • Offline content download for access without internet connectivity
  • Analytics dashboard for administrators and programme coordinators
Section 14
🟡 Source: DIKSHA

State Integration Model

📖 Est. reading time: 3 min

DIKSHA operates on a federated architecture that allows States and Union Territories to maintain their own content portals while being part of the national DIKSHA ecosystem. This ensures that States retain autonomy over State-specific curriculum content while benefiting from the shared national infrastructure.

State Integration Components

  • State-branded portal within the DIKSHA framework
  • State-specific curriculum content upload and management
  • State SCERT/SIE as content quality custodians for State content
  • State teacher registration and training management
  • State-level analytics and reporting
  • Integration with State Education Management Information Systems (EMIS)
  • Customisation of platform interface in State languages
The federated model of DIKSHA reflects the Constitutional principle of education being a concurrent subject. States have full autonomy over State-specific content while benefiting from the shared national technology infrastructure maintained by NCERT.

🟡 Source: DIKSHA

Content Governance

📖 Est. reading time: 3 min

DIKSHA follows a robust content governance framework to ensure that all content available on the platform is accurate, curriculum-aligned, age-appropriate, and of high quality.

Content Lifecycle Management

  • Creation: Content created by approved creators (teachers, subject experts, organisations)
  • Review: Multi-stage review by subject experts and pedagogical reviewers
  • Publication: Published on DIKSHA upon successful review
  • Discovery: Searchable and filterable by grade, subject, topic, medium, and board
  • Usage Tracking: Analytics on views, downloads, and ratings
  • Updation: Periodic review and update to ensure currency
  • Archival: Outdated content archived and replaced with updated versions

Digital Certification

📖 Est. reading time: 3 min

DIKSHA provides digital certificates to teachers and learners who successfully complete courses and training programmes on the platform. These certificates are verifiable, shareable, and stored in the learner's profile.

Certificate Features

  • Digitally signed certificates from NCERT, SCERT, or relevant issuing organisation
  • QR-code linked certificate verification
  • Downloadable in PDF format
  • Shareable on professional networks
  • Stored permanently in the learner's DIKSHA profile
  • Linked to specific course completion and assessment scores
Digital certificates from DIKSHA serve as recognised credentials for teacher professional development activities and can be used for career progression, performance appraisal, and professional portfolio documentation.

ICT in Schools

📖 Est. reading time: 4 min

The National Policy on ICT in School Education provides a comprehensive framework for the integration of Information and Communication Technology across all levels of school education in India. ICT in education is understood not merely as a tool for content delivery but as a transformative force for improving pedagogical quality, administrative efficiency, and equity of access.

ICT Integration Objectives

  • Provide ICT skills to all students as a core 21st-century competency
  • Use ICT as a pedagogical tool to enhance learning outcomes across subjects
  • Use ICT for educational administration and management at school, district, and state levels
  • Develop high-quality digital content in all Indian languages
  • Build teacher capacity in ICT use
  • Ensure equitable access to ICT for students in rural, hilly, and tribal areas

ICT@School Scheme

The ICT@School scheme provides financial support to States for establishment of ICT infrastructure in secondary and higher secondary schools. Under the scheme, schools receive computer labs with adequate hardware, software, networking, and internet connectivity. Teachers are trained in ICT integration, and digital content is made available through DIKSHA and other platforms.

ICT shall be treated as a cross-curricular competency rather than a standalone subject. The goal is to embed digital skills across all subject areas, ensuring that students develop technology proficiency in authentic learning contexts.

e-Content Development

📖 Est. reading time: 4 min

NCERT has developed a comprehensive e-content development framework that guides the creation of high-quality digital educational resources aligned with the national curriculum. e-Content development is a collaborative process involving subject experts, instructional designers, media specialists, and teachers.

Types of e-Content

  • e-Books: Interactive digital versions of NCERT textbooks with embedded multimedia
  • Video Lessons: Recorded instructional videos by subject experts
  • Animations: 2D and 3D animations explaining complex concepts
  • Simulations: Interactive simulations for Science and Mathematics
  • Audio Content: Podcasts, audio books, and narrated presentations
  • Assessment Items: Question banks, practice tests, and formative assessment tools
  • Lesson Plans: Structured teaching guides aligned with curriculum

Development Standards

All NCERT e-content follows the Sharable Content Object Reference Model (SCORM) and IMS standards to ensure interoperability across digital platforms. Content is tagged with comprehensive metadata — grade, subject, topic, learning outcome, difficulty level, and language — to enable effective discoverability and recommendation.

🟣 Source: NCERT/ICT

Digital Repository

📖 Est. reading time: 3 min

The National Repository of Open Educational Resources (NROER) is India's national digital repository of open educational resources for school education. It is maintained by NCERT and provides free access to thousands of digital learning resources for teachers and students.

NROER Features

  • Over 14,000 digital resources available freely
  • Content in multiple languages — Hindi, English, and regional languages
  • Resources across all grades (I to XII) and subjects
  • All resources are licensed under Creative Commons — freely shareable and usable
  • Search and filter by grade, subject, topic, medium, resource type, and language
  • Downloadable for offline access
  • Integration with DIKSHA for access through the national platform
Open licensing of educational resources on NROER ensures that teachers and students can legally use, modify, and share content. This promotes a culture of open knowledge sharing in education.

Online Assessment Systems

📖 Est. reading time: 4 min

Online and technology-enabled assessment systems are a critical component of the national educational technology framework. These systems support both formative assessment (assessment for learning) and summative assessment (assessment of learning) across all levels of school education.

Components of Online Assessment

  • Question banks aligned with national and State curriculum
  • Adaptive testing that adjusts difficulty based on learner performance
  • Multiple question formats: MCQ, fill-in-the-blank, drag-and-drop, short answer
  • Automated scoring for objective question types
  • Rubric-based assessment tools for subjective responses
  • Real-time feedback mechanisms for learners
  • Analytics dashboard for teachers to monitor student performance patterns

Educational Broadcasting

📖 Est. reading time: 3 min

Educational broadcasting through television and radio remains an important medium for reaching students in areas with limited internet connectivity. India has a rich tradition of educational broadcasting through Doordarshan and All India Radio, and this is being revitalised under the digital education framework.

PM eVIDYA Broadcasting

  • One Class One Channel: Dedicated Doordarshan channels for each grade (I to XII)
  • Swayam Prabha: 32 DTH channels for curriculum-based content
  • CBSE Podcast — Shiksha Vani: Educational podcasts for students and teachers
  • Community Radio: Leveraging community radio stations for local language content
  • Interactive Radio Instruction (IRI): Radio-based programmes with interactive elements
Educational broadcasting serves as a critical bridge for students in remote, hilly, and tribal areas where digital devices and internet connectivity may be unavailable, ensuring continuity of learning through accessible media.

Open Educational Resources

📖 Est. reading time: 3 min

Open Educational Resources (OER) are teaching, learning, and research materials that are freely available for anyone to use, repurpose, and redistribute under open licenses. India's national education framework strongly promotes the creation, adoption, and adaptation of OER as a strategy for democratising access to quality education.

OER Policy Framework

  • All publicly funded educational content shall be made available as OER
  • Creative Commons licensing shall be applied to all NCERT and DIKSHA content
  • States are encouraged to adopt OER licensing for State-produced content
  • NROER shall serve as the national OER repository for school education
  • Teacher-created content endorsed on DIKSHA shall be released as OER
  • International OER resources shall be adapted to Indian curriculum contexts

Digital Pedagogy

📖 Est. reading time: 4 min

Digital pedagogy refers to the practice of teaching and learning that is mediated by digital technologies. It is distinct from mere digitisation of content — it involves a fundamental rethinking of how teachers design learning experiences, how students engage with knowledge, and how assessment is conducted in digitally-mediated environments.

Principles of Digital Pedagogy

  • Learner-centredness: Digital tools facilitate personalised, self-paced learning
  • Active engagement: Technology enables simulations, collaborative projects, and inquiry-based learning
  • Multimodality: Content presented through multiple modes — text, video, audio, interactive — supports diverse learners
  • Formative feedback: Digital platforms enable continuous, real-time feedback on student progress
  • Collaboration: Digital tools facilitate peer collaboration across geographies
  • Reflective practice: Digital portfolios and learning journals support metacognitive development

TPACK Framework

Teachers are expected to develop Technological Pedagogical Content Knowledge (TPACK) — an integrated understanding of how technology, pedagogy, and subject content knowledge interact in effective digital teaching. Pre-service teacher education and in-service training programmes shall incorporate the TPACK framework explicitly.

Digital pedagogy is not about replacing effective teaching with technology — it is about amplifying the impact of excellent teaching through the strategic and purposeful use of digital tools and platforms.

Digital Equity & Inclusion

📖 Est. reading time: 3 min

Digital equity in education means ensuring that all students — regardless of their geographic location, socioeconomic status, gender, or disability — have fair and meaningful access to the digital tools, resources, and connectivity needed to fully participate in digitally-mediated learning.

Equity Dimensions

  • Access equity: Physical access to devices and connectivity
  • Skills equity: Digital literacy skills to use technology effectively
  • Content equity: Availability of culturally and linguistically relevant content
  • Participation equity: Meaningful engagement with digital learning, not passive consumption
  • Gender equity: Ensuring girls have equal access to and participation in digital learning
  • Disability inclusion: Assistive technology and accessible content for all learners

Cybersecurity in Education

📖 Est. reading time: 3 min

As schools and education systems increasingly move online, ensuring cybersecurity and protecting student data privacy become critical responsibilities. The national education framework recognises cybersecurity literacy as a core competency for students and mandates data protection standards for educational technology platforms.

Cybersecurity Education Components

  • Digital citizenship and responsible online behaviour
  • Understanding cyberthreats — phishing, malware, social engineering
  • Password management and account security
  • Privacy settings and data management on platforms
  • Safe and ethical use of social media
  • Reporting mechanisms for cyberbullying and online harassment
  • Intellectual property rights and fair use in the digital domain
Digital citizenship education shall be integrated into the school curriculum from the foundational stage, ensuring that students develop ethical, responsible, and safe online behaviour as a core life skill.

🟣 Source: NCERT/ICT

Data-Driven School Management

📖 Est. reading time: 3 min

The integration of ICT in educational administration enables data-driven decision-making at the school, district, and state levels. Management Information Systems (MIS) collect, process, and present data on enrolment, attendance, learning outcomes, teacher performance, and resource utilisation.

UDISE+ — Unified District Information System for Education Plus

UDISE+ is the national school information system covering over 1.5 million schools across India. It captures comprehensive data on:

  • Student enrolment by grade, gender, social category, and disability status
  • Teacher qualification, training, and deployment data
  • School infrastructure — buildings, classrooms, toilets, drinking water, ICT
  • Learning outcome data linked to assessment results
  • Financial data on school grants and expenditure

Data from UDISE+ informs policy decisions, resource allocation, and educational planning at all levels of government.

EdTech Ecosystem Framework

📖 Est. reading time: 5 min

India's EdTech ecosystem has grown significantly in recent years, driven by national policy initiatives, digital infrastructure investments, and a vibrant private sector. The national framework envisions a collaborative EdTech ecosystem in which government, academia, civil society, and industry work together to leverage technology for educational transformation.

Ecosystem Components

  • Government Platforms: DIKSHA, NROER, PM eVIDYA, SWAYAM, ePathshala
  • Academic Institutions: IITs, NCERT, SCERTs — for content, research, and innovation
  • EdTech Industry: Private sector platforms providing supplementary learning resources
  • Startups and Innovators: Emerging solutions in AI-based learning, vernacular content, and accessibility
  • Civil Society: NGOs and foundations working on digital equity and inclusion
  • International Partners: Collaborations for technology transfer and global best practices
EdTech Ecosystem Architecture

NETF as Ecosystem Coordinator

The National Educational Technology Forum (NETF) shall serve as the apex coordination body for the national EdTech ecosystem. It shall establish standards, evaluate technologies, fund research, and facilitate collaboration across all ecosystem stakeholders to ensure that the ecosystem serves the national educational goals of equity, access, and quality.

India's EdTech ecosystem, when aligned with national educational goals and driven by evidence-based policy, has the potential to transform learning outcomes for the 250 million children enrolled in India's school system — making it one of the most significant digital transformation initiatives in the world.
